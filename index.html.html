Impeachment qualitative evidence

The optimized search produced a reliable daily trend and high-engagement documents, but the requested cross-tab statistics returned an inconsistent global aggregation and were excluded. The strongest retrieved posts centered on:

  • the opening of the Senate impeachment trial;
  • whether the Vice President should personally appear;
  • the burden of proof and due process;
  • presentation of the alleged grave-threat video;
  • procedural disputes among senator-judges;
  • claims of political persecution versus constitutional accountability.

High-engagement amplifiers included Philippine Star, ABS-CBN News, GMA News, News5, INQUIRER.net, Radyo Pilipinas and political figures including Chel Diokno, Gerville Luistro, Sheila Sison, Pia Cayetano and Risa Hontiveros.

Benchmark conclusion

Has impeachment reached the same level of public outrage? On attention and velocity, yes—it has exceeded it. On corruption-specific disgust and institutional distrust tied to misuse of funds, the Discaya controversy remains the stronger benchmark.

Which caused greater direct reputational damage? The impeachment issue, because the allegations and trial are attached directly to Vice President Sara Duterte’s conduct, credibility and fitness for office.

Which caused greater damage to trust in government institutions? The Discaya controversy more directly undermined trust in procurement, infrastructure delivery and use of public funds, while impeachment creates broader but more partisan distrust in the House, Senate and political process.

Which is likely to remain active? Impeachment in the short term because every hearing can create a new spike; Discaya in the long term if prosecutions, audits, asset findings or infrastructure failures continue.

Tab 3 — Strategic Communications Implications

1. Separate fact from allegation

Every official communication should label verified records, allegations, evidence admitted in proceedings, contested claims and final findings separately.

2. Explain process, not personalities

Use plain-language explainers on Senate procedure, evidentiary standards, audit mechanisms, procurement controls and institutional responsibilities.

3. Demonstrate accountability

Publish timelines, responsible agencies, actions taken, documentary proof points and next decision dates.

Methodology, Limitations and Data Governance

Measured findings

  • Discaya peak date: 9 September 2025, 10,040 mentions.
  • VP impeachment peak date: 6 July 2026, 18,947 mentions.
  • Discaya 15-day window: 2–16 September 2025, 42,698 mentions, 2,847/day.
  • VP impeachment 15-day window: 26 June–10 July 2026, 63,131 mentions, 4,209/day.
  • Impeachment peak-day volume was about 89% higher; normalized daily average was about 48% higher.

Analyst interpretation

  • Discaya’s reputational mechanism is corruption, taxpayer loss, failed infrastructure and institutional impunity.
  • Impeachment’s mechanism is personal conduct, leadership credibility, legal accountability, political legitimacy and polarization.
  • Neutral automated sentiment should not be read as emotionally neutral; legal and factual reports can still carry serious negative reputational meaning.

Limitations

  • The Discaya standard saved-search folder could not be directly enumerated; a semantic query was used as a substitute.
  • The VP impeachment cross-tab statistics response was inconsistent with the optimized-search daily series and was excluded.
  • Reach is modeled and may not be additive across platforms or duplicate audiences.
  • Reposts, syndication and duplicate coverage could not be fully separated in every metric.
  • The impeachment window is right-truncated at 10 July 2026; future hearing dates may alter the peak and longevity assessment.
  • Emotion and narrative shares are analyst-coded from retrieved evidence and should not be represented as machine-measured percentages unless separately validated.
